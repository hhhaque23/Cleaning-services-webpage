# Pristine Cleaning Co.

## Register

brand

## Product Purpose

A home cleaning service for Rochester Hills and metro Detroit. Booking flips a normally annoying errand (phone tag, vague quotes, two-day reply windows) into something you order like food delivery. The site's job is to take a curious visitor and produce a confirmed booking in under sixty seconds, with no human contact required.

## Users

- **Busy professionals and families** in Rochester Hills and adjacent suburbs. Late 20s through 50s. Household income above $80k. Time is worth more than $40/hour to them. They are convenience shopping, not price shopping.
- **Move-in / move-out customers.** Renters wanting deposits back, homeowners staging a sale, landlords flipping units. Different urgency, same need: fast, reliable, no phone calls.

## Tone

Confident, direct, no marketing-speak. Honest about pricing. Writes like a competent neighbor, not a brand. Short sentences. The word "we" earns its place; uses "you" more often.

## Anti-references

- Existing local cleaning sites (2010-era contact forms, vague pricing, stock photos of someone in pearls vacuuming).
- Wellness / spa aesthetics. This is utilitarian premium, not lifestyle premium.
- Editorial-magazine layouts with italic serifs and drop caps. Wrong register.
- The "fresh & clean" trap: identical cyan-and-white minimalist landing pages every cleaning startup ships.

## Strategic principles

- **No friction.** Every section answers the silent objection that would make the visitor close the tab.
- **Trust is the product.** A stranger in your house. Real photos, real names, real insurance numbers, real guarantees with specific recovery paths.
- **Transparent pricing is the wedge.** Competitors hide it. Show it live, let the visitor change it themselves.
- **Subscription is the business.** One-time clients fund growth, recurring clients are the business. Always nudge toward biweekly after a one-off.
- **Same-day visible as inventory, not promise.** Empty slots are shown live, not hinted at.
